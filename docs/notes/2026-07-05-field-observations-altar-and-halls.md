## 1. Step count vs. hall capacity (observation)

- **Larger halls — capacity above ~500 — tend to have 7 steps.**
- **Smaller halls usually have 5 steps.**
- Not yet known whether an official recommendation exists tying capacity to
  step count (e.g. ≥500 capacity → 7 steps). **Open question for leadership.**

## 2. Tread dimensions — 5-step case (observation)

Counting from the **lowest** step upward:

| Step | Colour | Tread (going) observed |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (lowest) | Beige | ~**450 mm** |
| 2 | Beige | **1.1 – 1.3 m** (varies between halls) |
| 3 | Green | ~**300 mm** |
| 4 | Green | ~**300 mm** |
| 5 (top) | Green | **1.3 – 1.5 m** — this is the altar platform where the altar table stands |

**Corroboration:** the Osogbo ground floor plan
(`projects/GMF-OS-OSOGBO-01/04-Drawings/`) dimensions exactly this pattern —
450, 1,100, 300, 300, 1,300 — which supports adopting it as the standard
tread sequence. The variation observed (second tread 1.1–1.3 m; top tread
1.3–1.5 m) is the range to be closed at ratification.

For the **7-step case** (4 green + 3 beige per GMF-DS-001 v1.1 §3) the
corresponding tread sequence has **not yet been recorded** — to be measured
from a 7-step hall.

## 3. Altar table (observation)

- Observed dimensions: **~1.8 m wide × 850 or 750 mm deep** (dimensions taken
  at the table front).
- Corroboration: Osogbo plan shows 750 × 1,800.
- To ratify: 750 vs 850 depth.

## 4. Reading desk (observation)

- Placed on the **right-hand side of the altar** (viewed toward the altar).
- Stands on the **second step from the lowest** — i.e. the **second beige
  step** (the 1.1–1.3 m tread).

## 5. Altar gate, recess, curtains and internal cross (observation)

- Above the steps there is a **recess** in the altar wall — referred to as
  the **altar gate** — with a **coping band** running around it.
- **Curtains** are hung inside the recess.
- The **internal cross is hung inside this recess**, within the hall.

## 6. External cross (observation, incomplete)

- Most halls also carry a **cross outside** the building.
- There are said to be criteria for **where and how it may be angled** —
  this information is not yet available. **To be obtained from leadership.**

## 7. Proposed hall size tiers (author's proposal, not observed standard)

| Tier | Capacity | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | up to ~100 | typical small centre hall |
| Tier 2 | ~100 – 500 | medium halls |
| Tier 3 | ~500 and above | large halls; these host the **annual festival worship**, attended by worshippers from across the country |

If adopted, the tiers would anchor the reference-hall series in
GMF-BIM-001 §8 (template library) and possibly the 5-vs-7-step criterion
(§1 above).

## 8. Interior finishes (observation)

- The hall interior is **always white**.
- The main hall integrates the seating area and the altar area, but the two
  read differently: the **altar area is brilliant white**, the **seating
  area regular white**.

## 9. Ceiling — two levels with concealed altar lighting (observation)

- **Level 1** runs from the rear wall across the **seating area** and stops
  at the **beginning of the altar zone**.
- **Level 2** is **higher**, begins some distance **before** the altar and
  extends over the **entire altar area**.
- In the **gap between the two ceiling levels, lights are mounted pointing
  directly onto the altar** — i.e. onto the altar table.
- Consistent with GMF-DS-001 §04 (raised altar ceiling); adds the lighting
  detail, which should be written into §04 at ratification.

## 10. Attached buildings by hall scale (observation)

- **Smaller halls:** usually no attachments at all, or only a **small
  ante-room and/or flower room** (sometimes just a flower room with one
  exit).
- **Medium-scale halls:** tend to have **multiple attached offices within
  the same building**. In most observed cases of this type, the **main hall
  is on the first (upper) floor** and the attachments (offices etc.) are on
  the **ground floor**.
- Relevant to GMF-DS-001 §07 (adjoined buildings) and §11 (storey
  buildings) — note this observed pattern (hall above, offices below)
  differs from the §11 draft assumption and must be reconciled at
  ratification.

## Open questions arising

1. Is there an official rule tying **capacity to step count** (5 vs 7)?
2. What is the **7-step tread sequence**?
3. Altar table depth — **750 or 850 mm**?
4. **Second beige tread** and **top platform tread** — close the observed
   ranges (1.1–1.3 m; 1.3–1.5 m) to single values.
5. **External cross** — obtain the angling/placement criteria.
6. Are the **three capacity tiers** acceptable to leadership as the basis
   for the reference-hall series?
7. Confirm the **hall-above / offices-below** pattern as the sanctioned
   storey-building arrangement (GMF-DS-001 §11 currently assumes the hall
   on the topmost floor — consistent, but the entry rules need writing).
