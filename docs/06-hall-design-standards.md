# Hall of Worship Design Standards
**Grail Message Foundation — Building Projects**

| | |
|---|---|
| Document no. | GMF-DS-001 |
| Version | 1.0 (Draft for adoption — [TBR] values pending ratification) |
| Applies to | Design of all new Halls of Worship and modifications to existing halls |
| Status | Mandatory once adopted |

---

## 1. Purpose

Across multiple hall projects the same design elements have been built
differently — and sometimes wrongly — because no document fixed their
standard: the altar area and its steps, ceiling heights, the curved altar
wall, seating, the fore hall, and the finishes used in each location. The
Osogbo remodification (RPT-2026-OSG-001) is the direct cost of that gap.

This standard fixes **one set of design parameters for every Hall of
Worship**, so that every new hall is designed from these values and every
renovation is corrected toward them. It works together with GMF-PG-001
(phases), GMF-BIM-001 (parameters are built into every model and template)
and GMF-QS-001 (hold point 1 verifies them on site).

## 2. How to use this standard

1. Values marked **[TBR]** (*to be ratified*) are spiritual or
   Foundation-level decisions: confirmed by leadership, measured from the
   designated **reference hall**, entered here, and the document reissued as
   v1.1.
2. Values not marked [TBR] are proposed from the National Building Code and
   good practice, and apply unless amended at adoption.
3. **No deviation without approval** — any departure from a ratified value
   requires Board approval by change order (GMF-CM-001 §6) before execution.
4. All geometry is defined in the BIM model and set out from survey
   benchmarks — never set out by eye on site.

## 3. Altar area

All altar-zone geometry is governed by the **altar centreline** (centre of
altar wall through the middle of the hall); every dimension is set out from it.

| Parameter | Rule | Standard value |
|---|---|---|
| Number of altar steps | Identical in every hall; full width of altar opening; no winders/part-steps | **[TBR]** |
| Step dimensions | Risers equal ±5 mm; riser 150–170 mm; tread ≥ 300 mm; non-slip | riser [TBR], tread [TBR] |
| Step plan form & radius | Curved steps struck from the defined centre point on the altar centreline | R = **[TBR]** |
| Curved altar wall radius | Same centre point as steps — wall and steps concentric with the altar | R = **[TBR]** |
| Altar size | Platform depth × width fixed relative to hall width; altar itself per reference | platform [TBR], altar [TBR] |
| Reading desk | Standard size, top height, slope, position (offset from centreline, setback from step edge); detail in template library | **[TBR]** |
| Altar gate | Width, height, material, position on step line; opens away from congregation | **[TBR]** |
| Cross location (internal) | Centred on altar centreline on the altar wall; fixed mounting height; concealed fixing | height **[TBR]** |

**Site rule:** the altar zone is set out and checked at hold point 1
(GMF-QS-001 §4) against the BIM model before any foundation or step is cast.

## 4. Ceiling

| Parameter | Rule | Standard value |
|---|---|---|
| Main hall clear height | Finished floor to finished ceiling | min. 3.6 m (proposed) |
| Altar section ceiling | Raised above main hall ceiling over the full altar zone, stepped at the altar opening line | +600 mm (proposed, [TBR]) |
| Fore hall ceiling | Equal to or lower than main hall — never higher | = main hall (proposed) |
| Build-up | Plasterboard on stud/track, flush jointed; access panels outside altar zone; no exposed services in worship space | 10 mm board |

## 5. Fore hall

| Parameter | Rule | Standard value |
|---|---|---|
| Provision | Every hall has a fore hall between entrance and main hall — no direct entry from outside | mandatory |
| Ratio to main hall | Fore hall floor area : main hall area, constant across capacities | 1 : 5 (proposed, [TBR]) |
| Separation | Full-height partition to ceiling; doors on the centreline axis; silent-closing; acoustically sealed | full height |

## 6. Capacity, seating and hall size

| Parameter | Rule | Standard value |
|---|---|---|
| Participants-to-area ratio | Net seating area per seated participant (excl. altar zone, aisles, fore hall). Hall size derives from congregation + growth — never the reverse | 0.65 m²/person (proposed) |
| Row spacing | Seat back to seat back, constant | 950 mm (proposed) |
| Aisles | Central aisle on the altar centreline; side aisles at walls; no seat >6 seats from an aisle | centre ≥1200 mm, side ≥900 mm |
| Front row to altar steps | Clear distance, constant across halls | **[TBR]** |
| Arrangement | Symmetrical about centreline; rows parallel to altar wall; Foundation seating convention recorded at ratification | **[TBR]** |

## 7. Adjoined buildings and rooms

- **Allowed adjoining spaces:** ante-room and flower room (flanking the
  altar), fore hall, conveniences, cloakroom, administration/reception —
  subject to the ratified list **[TBR]**. No residential, commercial or
  kitchen space adjoins the worship hall directly.
- **Entry requirements:** ancillary spaces never open directly into the main
  hall except the ante-room/flower room doors serving the altar and the fore
  hall doors. Conveniences are reached from outside the worship space, never
  through the main hall, never against the altar wall.
- **Acoustic separation:** full-height sealed walls between hall and occupied
  ancillary spaces; generator/pump rooms detached or remote.

## 11. Halls in storey buildings

1. **Permission:** requires explicit Board approval at Gate 1 with recorded
   justification.
2. **Placement:** worship hall on the topmost floor of its wing unless
   leadership ratifies otherwise — no occupied rooms, sanitary spaces or
   tanks directly above the worship space, never above the altar zone [TBR].
3. **Full standard applies:** altar geometry (§3), ceilings (§4) and fore
   hall (§5) unchanged — the structure is designed around the hall.
4. **Structure & vibration:** long-span over the hall (no columns in the
   worship space); vibration and acoustic transmission checked.
5. **Access & egress:** stairs and travel distances sized for full capacity
   per the National Building Code; level access/lift for elderly and
   disabled worshippers.
6. **Services:** no water/drainage pipework above the worship space; other
   occupancies acoustically isolated.

## 12. Ratification and deviation

1. Leadership designates the **reference hall**; all [TBR] values are
   measured/decided from it, entered here and in the BIM template library;
   the standard is reissued as v1.1.
2. From v1.1 the values bind every project; project-specific deviations need
   Board approval by change order **before** execution.
3. Existing halls are assessed against this standard when their as-built BIM
   models are produced (GMF-HR-001 §4); differences are logged and corrected
   at renovation.
