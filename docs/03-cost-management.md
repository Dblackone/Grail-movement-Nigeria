# Cost Management & Procurement
**Grail Movement Nigeria — Building Projects**

| | |
|---|---|
| Document no. | GMN-CM-001 |
| Version | 1.0 (Draft for adoption) |
| Applies to | All expenditure on Halls of Worship and ancillary building projects |
| Status | Mandatory once adopted |

---

## 1. Purpose

Past projects suffered unnecessary cost overruns because there was no common
rule for estimating, tendering, paying and controlling changes. This document
sets those rules. The core principles:

1. **No budget, no project.** Nothing is bought and no one is mobilised before
   the relevant gate budget is approved.
2. **Quantities come from the model.** The BOQ is taken off the BIM model
   (GMN-BIM-001), which prevents both under-ordering and padding.
3. **Competition by default.** Works and major supplies are tendered.
4. **Every naira is traceable.** Payment only against certificates, invoices
   and approved change orders.

## 2. Cost Planning Through the Phases

| Phase / Gate | Estimate class | Accuracy | Prepared from |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gate 0 (Charter) | Class E | order of magnitude | benchmark ₦/m² rates from previous halls |
| Gate 1 (Feasibility) | Class D | ±30% | capacity brief + site conditions |
| Concept design | Class C | ±20% | LOD 200 model quantities |
| Gate 2 (Technical design) | **Class B** | ±10% | LOD 300 model take-off, priced BOQ |
| Gate 3 (Contract award) | Class A | contract sum | tendered prices |

Rules:
- Every estimate includes a **contingency**: 15% at Class D, 10% at Class B,
  5% on contract award. Contingency is spent only through change orders.
- Every estimate includes **inflation/escalation allowance** appropriate to
  the programme length — material prices in Nigeria are volatile; a project
  planned across more than 12 months must state its escalation assumption.
- If a design-stage estimate exceeds the approved envelope, the **design is
  value-engineered back into budget** at that stage. Discovering the overrun
  on site is a failure of this procedure.
- Benchmark rates (₦/m² by element) from every completed project are recorded
  in the lessons-learned report and used for the next Class E/D estimate.

## 3. The Bill of Quantities (BOQ)

- Produced by the QS from the LOD 300 model at Gate 2, in elemental format
  (substructure, frame, walls, roof, finishes, services, external works,
  preliminaries).
- Spot-checked manually per GMN-BIM-001 §6.
- The priced BOQ is the basis of tender comparison, interim valuations and
  the final account. Lump-sum "provisional" items are kept to a minimum and
  each must be listed and justified.

## 4. Procurement Rules

| Value band (set and reviewed by the Board) | Method |
|---|---|
| Minor purchases below the petty threshold | Direct purchase, receipt filed |
| Above petty threshold, below tender threshold | Minimum **3 written quotations**, comparison sheet filed |
| Above tender threshold (main works, roofing, MEP packages) | **Competitive tender, minimum 3 bidders**, sealed submissions, opened and minuted together |

Mandatory controls:
- Tender evaluation uses a written comparison (price, completeness against
  BOQ, capability, programme) signed by QS and PM, recommended to the Board.
- **Conflict of interest must be declared** — no member of the project team
  or Board may have an undisclosed interest in a bidder.
- Contract is signed before mobilisation. Mobilisation/advance payment must
  not exceed the percentage fixed by the Board and must be secured
  (bank guarantee or equivalent) above the threshold the Board sets.
- Direct negotiation instead of tender requires a written Board waiver stating
  the reason (e.g. genuine sole source, emergency).

## 5. Payments

The three-signature chain (GMN-PG-001 §3) applies to every payment:

1. **Certify** — Site Supervisor confirms the work/materials exist on site.
2. **Value & recommend** — QS values the work against the BOQ and issues an
   interim certificate (monthly).
3. **Approve & pay** — PM approves within delegated limit; Board approves
   above it. Payment is by bank transfer; cash payments only below the petty
   threshold.

Standard contract terms:
- **Retention:** 10% of each valuation, capped at 5% of contract sum; half
  released at practical completion, half after the defects liability period.
- **Defects liability period:** minimum 6 months.
- Materials on site may be certified only if stored securely and vested in
  the project.

## 6. Change Control

The single biggest historical source of overrun. Rules:

1. **No verbal variations.** Only a written
   [Change Order](../templates/change-order.md) signed per the payment chain
   creates a cost or scope change.
2. Every change order states: reason, cost effect (priced by QS against BOQ
   rates), time effect, and which budget line (contingency) funds it.
3. Changes above the Board threshold require Board approval **before** the
   work is done, not after.
4. The QS maintains a change register; the monthly cost report shows
   approved changes, pending changes and remaining contingency.

## 7. Monthly Cost Report

Issued by the QS to the PM and Board every month during Phases 3–5:

- Approved budget vs. certified to date vs. **forecast final cost**,
- contingency drawdown and remaining,
- change register summary,
- cash-flow forecast for the next 3 months (so funds are raised before, not
  after, they are needed).

Forecast final cost exceeding budget by more than 5% is an exception event
(GMN-PG-001 §5) and goes to the Board within 7 days.

## 8. Final Account

Within 90 days of practical completion the QS agrees the final account with
the Contractor: measured works, approved change orders, releases of
retention, and any claims. The final account, with the as-built records
(GMN-BIM-001 §10), closes the project at Gate 5.
