**Grail Movement Nigeria — Building Projects**

| | |

---

## 1. Purpose

Weak supervision made past projects hard to control. This document defines
**who watches the work, what they check, and how problems are escalated** so
that every hall is built to the same benchmark.

## 2. Supervision Structure

- A **Site Supervisor / Clerk of Works** is appointed for every active site
  and is present whenever critical work is executed. They report to the PM —
  **never** employed by or paid through the Contractor.
- The **design engineers** (structural, MEP) perform stage inspections at the
  hold points in Section 4 and confirm in writing.
- The **PM** visits site at least monthly and before every gate.

## 3. Daily and Weekly Records

| Record | Content | Kept by |
|---|---|---|
| **Site diary** (daily) | Weather, labour count, plant on site, work done, deliveries, visitors, instructions given, incidents | Site Supervisor |
| **[Weekly site report](../templates/site-report.md)** | Progress vs. programme, photos (minimum 6, dated, consistent viewpoints), issues, look-ahead | Site Supervisor → PM |
| **Site instruction book** | Numbered written instructions to the Contractor (no cost effect without a change order per GMN-CM-001 §6) | Site Supervisor |
| **Materials register** | Deliveries with quantities, source, and test/quality certificates | Site Supervisor |

## 4. Mandatory Hold Points

Work **must not proceed past a hold point** until inspected and released in
writing on the [inspection checklist](../templates/inspection-checklist.md):

| # | Hold point | Released by |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Setting-out — dimensions and orientation checked against drawings and survey beacons | Site Supervisor + Architect |
| 2 | Foundation excavation — depth and bearing stratum vs. soil report | Structural Engineer |
| 3 | Foundation reinforcement — size, spacing, cover, before any pour | Structural Engineer |
| 4 | Every structural concrete pour (columns, beams, slabs) — formwork, rebar, mix | Structural Engineer or delegated Supervisor |
| 5 | DPC / ground floor level before blockwork proceeds | Site Supervisor |
| 6 | Roof structure before covering — member sizes, connections, anchorage | Structural Engineer |
| 7 | Services first fix before covering up (conduits, plumbing in walls/slabs) | MEP Engineer |
| 8 | Waterproofing / tanking before backfill or screed | Site Supervisor |
| 9 | Practical completion inspection — full snag list | PM + Design Lead + QS |

## 5. Materials and Workmanship Benchmarks

All materials comply with the relevant **SON (Standards Organisation of
Nigeria)** standards. Minimum controls:

- **Cement:** SON-certified brands only; stored dry off the ground; no
  set/lumpy cement used.
- **Reinforcement:** mill certificates required per delivery; visibly
  corroded or undersized bars rejected. Spot-check diameters with calipers.
- **Concrete:** mix design approved by the Structural Engineer before first
  pour; cube tests (or engineer-approved equivalent) for structural pours —
  minimum one set per pour day; curing enforced for at least 7 days.
- **Blocks:** from an approved supplier or site-moulded under supervision;
  crushing test on samples per source; blocks cured before laying.
- **Sand/aggregate:** free of organic material and excessive silt (bottle
  test on each new source).
- **Roofing, doors, windows, finishes:** samples approved and retained before
  bulk purchase; installed work is compared against the retained sample.
- **Electrical:** cables and gear SON-certified; all circuits tested and a
  test certificate issued before energising.
- **Water/plumbing:** pressure test before covering; tanks and borehole
  installations per manufacturer's data filed in handover records.

Workmanship reference: the drawings and specification extracted from the BIM
model are the standard. Where a detail is not specified, the National
Building Code applies, and the Design Lead decides.

## 6. Health, Safety and Site Conduct

- Contractor provides and enforces basic PPE (helmets, boots) for all
  workers; scaffolding must be sound and inspected weekly.
- Contractor's all-risk insurance and workmen's compensation must be valid
  before site possession and remain valid throughout (checked monthly by PM).
- Site secured against unauthorised entry; materials store lockable with a
  signed issue register.
- Any injury requiring medical attention, structural distress, or statutory
  stop-order is reported to the PM the **same day** and is an exception event
  under GMN-PG-001 §5.

## 7. Non-Conformance and Escalation

1. Defective work is recorded in a **non-conformance note** (what, where,
   drawing reference, photo).
2. Contractor proposes remedy; the relevant engineer approves it.
3. Rework is at the Contractor's cost unless a change order says otherwise.
4. Repeated non-conformance (3 notes on the same trade) triggers a formal
   warning by the PM and is reported to the Board.
5. **Cover-ups without inspection release** (Section 4) are treated as
   non-conforming and may be ordered opened up at the Contractor's cost.

## 8. Practical Completion and Defects

- Practical completion only when: hold point 9 inspection passed, snag list
  agreed with completion dates, test certificates delivered, site cleared.
- During the defects liability period the Site Supervisor logs defects
  reported by hall users; the Contractor remedies within agreed timescales;
  retention is released only after the final defects inspection (Gate 5).
