# Project Build Guidelines
**Grail Movement Nigeria — Building Projects**

| | |
|---|---|
| Document no. | GMN-PG-001 |
| Version | 1.0 (Draft for adoption) |
| Applies to | All Halls of Worship and ancillary buildings in Nigeria |
| Status | Mandatory once adopted |

---

## 1. Purpose

This guideline defines **how every Grail Movement building project in Nigeria
is initiated, planned, designed, built and handed over**. Its objectives are:

1. **Cost-effectiveness** — no project starts without a realistic budget, and
   no money is spent outside approved procedures.
2. **Consistent standards** — every hall meets the same quality benchmark
   regardless of location.
3. **Proper supervision and control** — clear roles, clear reporting, and a
   documented trail for every decision.
4. **Reusability** — designs, models and documents from one project accelerate
   the next.

No project may proceed to a new phase without completing the **gate approval**
of the previous phase (Section 4).

## 2. Scope

Applies to:
- New Halls of Worship,
- Extensions, renovations and major repairs of existing halls,
- Ancillary buildings (administration, accommodation, utilities) on hall sites.

Minor maintenance below the threshold set by the Project Board (Section 3) is
exempt but must still be recorded in the site logbook.

## 3. Roles and Responsibilities

Every project must have the following roles filled **in writing** before Phase
1 is approved. One person may hold more than one role only where expressly
permitted below.

| Role | Responsibility | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Project Board** | Approves budget, phase gates, and changes above the change threshold. Owns the project. | Minimum 3 members. Represents Grail Movement Nigeria leadership. |
| **Project Manager (PM)** | Day-to-day management: programme, budget tracking, coordination, reporting to the Board. | Must not also be the Contractor. |
| **Design Lead / Architect** | Design, drawings and BIM model per [GMN-BIM-001](02-bim-guidelines.md). | Registered with ARCON where the law requires. |
| **Structural / MEP Engineers** | Engineering design and construction-stage checks. | COREN-registered where the law requires. |
| **Quantity Surveyor (QS)** | Bill of quantities, cost plan, valuations, cost reporting per [GMN-CM-001](03-cost-management.md). | Independent from the Contractor. |
| **Site Supervisor / Clerk of Works** | Daily site presence, inspections and reports per [GMN-QS-001](04-quality-and-supervision.md). | Reports to the PM, not to the Contractor. |
| **Contractor(s)** | Execution of the works under contract. | Selected per the procurement rules in GMN-CM-001. |

**Key control:** the person who approves payment must never be the same
person who certifies the work, and neither may be the Contractor. This
three-way separation (certify → recommend → approve) is mandatory.

## 4. Project Phases and Gate Approvals

Every project passes through six phases. Each phase ends with a **gate**: a
documented approval by the Project Board. Skipping a gate is a breach of this
guideline.

### Phase 0 — Initiation
- Need identified (new hall, extension, repair).
- **Deliverable:** [Project Charter](../templates/project-charter.md) —
  purpose, location, rough capacity, order-of-magnitude cost, funding source,
  proposed team.
- **Gate 0:** Board approves the Charter and releases feasibility funds only.

### Phase 1 — Feasibility & Land
- Confirm land title/documents (C of O, deed, survey plan) and site suitability
  (soil investigation, access, utilities, flooding, topographic survey).
- Preliminary capacity brief: congregation size, growth allowance, ancillary
  needs.
- Budget estimate (Class D, ±30%) prepared by the QS.
- **Gate 1:** Board approves site, brief and budget envelope.

### Phase 2 — Design
- Concept design → developed design → technical design, each reviewed against
  the brief and the cost plan.
- Full BIM model produced per [GMN-BIM-001](02-bim-guidelines.md); drawings
  are extracted from the model, never drawn separately.
- Statutory approvals obtained: state development permit / building approval,
  environmental requirements, fire service where applicable.
- QS updates the cost plan at each design stage; design must be **re-worked,
  not the budget silently increased**, if the estimate exceeds the envelope.
- **Gate 2:** Board approves final design, cost plan (Class B, ±10%), and
  authority approvals in hand.

### Phase 3 — Procurement
- Bill of Quantities produced from the BIM model.
- Contractor selection by competitive tender per
  [GMN-CM-001 §4](03-cost-management.md) — minimum three bids for works above
  the tender threshold.
- Contract signed **before** any mobilisation payment; contract must define
  scope, price, programme, retention, defects liability period and
  variation procedure.
- **Gate 3:** Board approves contract award and full construction budget.

### Phase 4 — Construction
- Works executed under continuous supervision per
  [GMN-QS-001](04-quality-and-supervision.md).
- Monthly: valuation by QS, progress report by PM to the Board, updated cost
  forecast.
- All changes go through the **change order procedure** — no verbal
  instructions to the Contractor carry cost or scope effect.
- **Gate 4:** Practical completion certified — snag list issued, hall usable.

### Phase 5 — Handover & Close-out
- Defects liability period (minimum 6 months) with retention held.
- As-built BIM model and documents delivered and archived in this repository
  structure.
- Final account agreed and closed by the QS.
- **Lessons-learned report** written by the PM and filed — this is how the
  guideline itself improves.
- **Gate 5:** Board closes the project and releases final retention.

## 5. Reporting and Control Rhythm

| Report | By | To | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Site diary | Site Supervisor | PM | Daily |
| [Site progress report](../templates/site-report.md) | Site Supervisor | PM | Weekly |
| Cost report & forecast | QS | PM & Board | Monthly |
| Project status report | PM | Board | Monthly |
| Gate submission | PM | Board | At each gate |

A project is **in exception** (Board must be notified within 7 days) if:
- forecast final cost exceeds approved budget by more than 5%, or
- the programme slips more than 4 weeks, or
- a safety incident, structural concern or statutory stop-order occurs.

## 6. Documentation Rules

1. Every project gets a project code: `GMN-<STATE>-<TOWN>-<NN>` (e.g.
   `GMN-EN-ENUGU-01`) — used on all files, drawings and correspondence.
2. All project documents are filed in the standard folder structure defined in
   [GMN-BIM-001 §7](02-bim-guidelines.md).
3. Decisions of the Board are minuted; instructions to contractors are written
   (site instruction forms); payments reference certificates.
4. As-built records are a condition of final payment to the Contractor.

## 7. Compliance with Nigerian Law and Standards

All projects must comply with, as applicable:
- The **National Building Code** and the building/planning regulations of the
  state where the site is located (development permit before construction).
- Professional registration laws: **ARCON** (architects), **COREN**
  (engineers), **QSRBN** (quantity surveyors) for roles the law reserves.
- **Standards Organisation of Nigeria (SON)** standards for materials
  (cement, reinforcement, blocks, roofing) — material certificates are checked
  under GMN-QS-001.
- Labour, safety and insurance requirements, including contractor's all-risk
  insurance and workmen's compensation before site possession.
