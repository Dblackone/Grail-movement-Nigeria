01. Executive Summary

This report presents the scope of work and an approximate cost estimate for the modification of the altar and the side of the structure at the Hall of Worship, Osogbo. Five work items are covered: correction of the altar steps (with associated door and window relocation), structural changes to the side walls at the Ante-Room and Flower Room, ceiling installation, a new dividing partition between the Fore Hall and Main Hall, and the curved altar wall. The estimated project total is ₦16,137,000, inclusive of allowances and a 10% contingency. All figures are prepared for planning purposes only and do not constitute a tender or contract sum.

02. Background

The works are based on the project drawings (Ground Floor Plan, Longitudinal Section SO-1 and Altar Elevation S-02) read together with on-site measurement and take-offs. Site re-measurement found the building approximately 0.6 m shorter than drawn along the Lobby–Altar axis, which directly affects the altar zone and triggered the decision to relocate the altar-side doors. On site, the Fore Hall and Main Hall have not yet been demarcated; the dividing wall will be built as drywall throughout in place of the blockwork proposed in the plan. Rates used in the estimate are installed rates (material and labour, measured on one-side surface area) obtained for this project, with cement taken at ₦12,500 per bag.

03. Findings

01
The building is approximately 0.6 metres shorter on site than what is shown on the plan (15.246 m measured versus 15.840 m on plan, measured from the altar wall to the lobby wall). The existing altar measures 3.00 m, compared to 3.45 m on the approved plan.
02
The corrected altar step layout requires the altar side doors to be relocated approximately 0.9 m toward the lobby wall. One window section will be removed, and the door relocated into the former window position. In total, the altar step modification will affect two (2) doors and two (2) windows. Additionally, due to the structural modifications on both the left and right sides of the building, two (2) additional doors and six (6) additional windows will be removed, bringing the overall total to four (4) doors and eight (8) windows affected.
03
The Fore Hall / Main Hall dividing partition does not exist on site. It will be constructed as full-height drywall (10 mm cement board on stud and track) in place of the blockwork shown on the plan.
04
The existing structural columns in the side wall carry the roof and roof slab. They must not be cut until temporary beam support is in place; this is provided for under Allowance A1.

04. Scope of Works and Construction Sequence

1.
Altar steps — demolish the existing five steps, mark out and cast new corrected steps (DPC membrane, formwork, concrete and finishing). Relocate the affected doors and windows: the altar-side door moves approximately 0.9 m into the window position.
2.
Side wall foundation and structure (Ante-Room + Flower Room) — excavate and blind, foundation blockwork, reinforcement from foundation to superstructure, formwork and concrete to columns, beams and lintels. Existing structural columns are supported temporarily before removal (Allowance A1); mason dresses, plasters and reinstalls doors and windows.
3.
Ceiling installation — 10 mm plasterboard on stud and track: first the Main Hall + Fore Hall (117 sqm), then the Altar section (39 sqm), raised approximately 600 mm above the Main Hall ceiling.
4.
New partition wall (Fore Hall / Main Hall) — full-height drywall, 10 mm cement board on 75 mm stud and track, 60 sqm one-side surface area, built up to the new ceiling.
5.
Altar curve wall — single-board curved wall, 10 mm plasterboard on 60 mm stud and track, 65 sqm, following the curved apse geometry from the Ante-Room corner to the Flower Room corner.

06. Recommendations

  1. Before demolition of any column, ensure that the temporary beam supports are ready and in place — this is safety-critical.
  2. A re-survey of the altar zone should be carried out before any work commences, due to the 0.6 m discrepancy between the plan and on-site measurements.
  3. The rates for the drywall and ceiling works are general contractor rates. The best approach to save cost is to purchase the materials directly and invite the contractor to install only — from experience this saves 15–20% of the cost.
  4. Before commencement of any work, this estimate should be converted to a Bill of Quantities with actual costs from all vendors and the contractor.

07. Conclusion

The scope is well defined and sequenced, and the estimate is built from project-specific installed rates and measured areas. At this level of definition, the recommended planning budget is ₦16,137,000 including the 10% contingency, excluding VAT and professional fees. The next step is to complete the site confirmations listed under Recommendations and obtain the contractor's firm quotation against this scope.
