Purpose

This report translates the Proposed Drawing against the As-Built Drawing for the Hall of Worship, Osogbo, and sets out the resulting scope of work. It is limited to what the two drawings show — the building as surveyed, and the building as proposed — and does not cover cost, methodology or safety, which are addressed elsewhere (cost: report RPT-2026-OSG-001). Its purpose is to give a plain, illustrated account of what changes and what must be built.

The Two Drawings

Both drawings are reproduced below at full size for reference. Every other figure in this report is a cropped extract from one of these two sheets.

Existing
Figure 1. As-Built Drawing — Lobby, Fore Hall / Main Hall (undivided), Ante-Room, Flower Room and the existing altar apse.
Proposed
Figure 2. Proposed Drawing — reconstructed altar, relocated openings, extended Ante-Room and Flower Room, new columns, and new Fore Hall / Main Hall partition.
Reading the drawings: red marks the As-Built items to be removed; grey fill marks walls to be demolished; blue marks new walls and doors on the Proposed Drawing; pink marks new reinforced concrete columns. The building envelope along the Lobby–Main Hall axis (15.246 m) is unchanged — every change is contained within three zones: the altar apse, the Ante-Room / Flower Room corners, and the Fore Hall / Main Hall boundary.

1. Altar

Existing
Figure 3. Existing altar apse — chord 3000–3015 mm.
Proposed
Figure 4. Proposed altar apse — chord widened to 3450 mm.

The altar chord widens from approximately 3.00–3.015 m to 3.45 m, an increase of roughly 450 mm. The radial depth to the Ante-Room-side corridor (3000 mm) is unchanged. The curve shown is diagrammatic; exact setting-out is a working-drawing item.

Work to be Done
  • Demolish the existing altar platform and steps in full.
  • Reconstruct the altar wall and steps to the widened 3.45 m chord.
  • Confirm final radius, centre point and step dimensions before construction.

2. Doors and Windows

Existing
Figure 5. Existing north wall — altar-side door and window marked red for removal.
Proposed
Figure 6. Proposed north wall — altar-side door relocated; new partition door (blue).

Four doors are affected in total: two altar-side doors relocate approximately 0.9 m toward the Lobby wall (into a former window bay, since the widened altar would otherwise clash with their existing position), and two doors at the Ante-Room / Flower Room corridor entrances are replaced with wider openings in the new extended wall. Eight windows are affected in total — one absorbed into each relocated altar-side door, and the remainder lost to, and reconstructed within, the two extended walls.

Work to be Done
  • Brick up the two existing altar-side door openings; form new openings 0.9 m toward the Lobby wall.
  • Demolish and rebuild the two Ante-Room/Flower Room corridor doors as wider openings.
  • Remove eight windows across the altar and extension zones; reconstruct within the new wall lines.
  • Form two new door openings within the new Fore Hall / Main Hall partition.

3. Ante-Room and Flower Room Extensions

Proposed
Figure 7. Ante-Room extended approx. 703 mm beyond the existing line; new column (pink) and window.
Proposed
Figure 8. Flower Room extended approx. 728 mm beyond the existing line; setting-out chain noted at right.

Both rooms are minimal, undimensioned corner spaces on the As-Built drawing. The Proposed Drawing extends each outward — Ante-Room to a depth of 3394 mm, Flower Room to 3381 mm — on a new wall built from ground level and tied into a new foundation, per the drawing's own construction note.

Work to be Done
  • Demolish the existing corner-enclosing walls at both rooms (grey fill on As-Built drawing).
  • Excavate and cast new foundations, tied into the existing building.
  • Build new blockwork walls, doors and windows to the extended lines shown.
  • Cast new corner columns and tie the roof into the extended footprint.

4. Columns

Four existing columns, embedded in the perimeter wall flanking the altar, are demolished — their positions are superseded by the widened apse and extended side walls. Four new reinforced concrete columns are built at the outer corners of the two extensions (pink markings on the Proposed Drawing).

As noted on the As-Built drawing: these columns carry roof load and must not be cut until temporary beam support is in place.
Work to be Done
  • Install temporary beam support before any existing column is cut.
  • Demolish the four existing columns once support is confirmed and new foundations are ready.
  • Cast four new reinforced concrete columns with pad footings at the extension corners.

5. Fore Hall / Main Hall Partition

Proposed
Figure 9. New partition location — 3450 mm from the west wall / 10517 mm from the Lobby wall, with the drawing's own drywall specification note.

No partition exists on site today; the Fore Hall and Main Hall currently read as one undivided volume. The Proposed Drawing calls for a new partition, per its own note: 100 mm thick, 75 mm steel track and stud, 10 mm cement board both faces, with 2″×3″ or 2″×4″ timber reinforcement at door jambs.

Work to be Done
  • Set out and build the new drywall partition per the drawing's specification.
  • Form one new door opening within the partition.

Next Step

This comparison is based solely on the As-Built and Proposed drawings. Before construction, the altar geometry, extension setting-out and partition detail should be developed into a full Construction Working Drawing Package with confirmed dimensions, levels and reinforcement.

A more detailed CAD ground floor plan already exists at projects/GMF-OS-OSOGBO-01/04-Drawings/GMF-OS-OSOGBO-01-AR-101-ground-floor-plan.pdf, including surveyed altar setting-out (radius, station point and angles). It was not used as a source for this report, which is limited to the As-Built/Proposed pair above — it should be reconciled with this comparison before the Working Drawing Package is produced.
