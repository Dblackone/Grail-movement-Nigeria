01. Purpose

Across multiple hall projects the same design elements have been built differently — and sometimes wrongly — because no document fixed their standard: the altar area and its steps, ceiling heights, the curved altar wall, seating, the fore hall, and the finishes used in each location. The Osogbo remodification (RPT-2026-OSG-001) is the direct cost of that gap. This standard fixes one set of design parameters for every Hall of Worship, so that every new hall is designed from these values and every renovation is corrected toward them.

This document works together with GMF-PG-001 (project phases), GMF-BIM-001 (the parameters below are built into every BIM model and template) and GMF-QS-001 (hold point 1 — setting-out — verifies them on site).

02. How to Use This Standard

1.
Each section defines the rule and, where applicable, a standard value. Values marked [TBR] — to be ratified — are spiritual or Foundation-level decisions: they must be confirmed by leadership, measured from the designated reference hall, entered here, and the document reissued as v1.1.
2.
Values not marked [TBR] are proposed from the National Building Code and good practice, and apply unless leadership amends them at adoption.
3.
No deviation without approval. Any departure from a ratified value on any project requires Project Board approval through the change-order procedure (GMF-CM-001 §6) before execution — never a site decision.
4.
All geometry in this standard is defined in the project BIM model and set out from the survey benchmarks — never set out by eye on site.

11. Halls in Storey Buildings

Where a Hall of Worship forms part of a multi-storey building, the following apply in addition to all sections above:

1.
Permission: a hall in a storey building requires explicit Board approval at Gate 1, with the justification recorded (typically urban land constraints).
2.
Placement: the worship hall occupies the topmost floor of its wing unless leadership ratifies otherwise — no occupied rooms, sanitary spaces or water tanks directly above the worship space, and never above the altar zone [TBR].
3.
Full standard applies: the altar geometry (§03), ceiling heights (§04) and fore hall (§05) apply unchanged — the structural grid and floor-to-floor height must be designed around the hall, not the hall squeezed into a standard floor.
4.
Structure & vibration: long-span structure over the hall (no columns within the worship space); floor design checked for vibration and acoustic transmission from other floors.
5.
Access & egress: stair widths and travel distances sized for the full hall capacity per the National Building Code; level access or lift provision for elderly and disabled worshippers.
6.
Services: no water or drainage pipework routed above the worship space; other occupancies in the building acoustically isolated from the hall.

12. Ratification and Deviation

1.
Leadership designates the reference hall; all [TBR] values are measured/decided from it, entered into this document and into the BIM template library, and the standard is reissued as v1.1.
2.
From v1.1 the values are binding on every project. A deviation on a specific project requires Board approval by change order (GMF-CM-001 §6) before execution, recorded in this document's project file.
3.
Existing halls are assessed against this standard when their as-built BIM models are produced (GMF-HR-001 §4); differences are logged and corrected when renovation works are undertaken.
